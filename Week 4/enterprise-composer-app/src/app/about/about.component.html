Unsuk Chin
Biography

Unsuk Chin (born in 1961) is a South Korean Composer of classical music. Ms. Chin taught herself how to play the piano and music theory at a young age. She moved to Germany in 1985 where she studied with Sukhi Kang in Seoul and Gyorgy Ligeti in Hamburg. Ms. Chin's career has been very successful, and she has received multiple awards from the Gaudeamus Award in 1985 to the Bach Prize 2019. Unsuk Chin founded the Seoul Philharmonic Orchestra's Ars Nova series, and serves as artistic director of the Music of Todayseries at London's Philharmonia Orchestra. You can find her works published exclusively by Boosey & Hawkes.
